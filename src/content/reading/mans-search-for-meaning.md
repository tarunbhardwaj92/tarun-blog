Some books give information. Others rearrange the way you look at life.

What stayed with me most was Frankl’s idea that meaning is not something we invent intellectually. It is something life demands from us through suffering, responsibility, and choice.

The book quietly challenges modern assumptions about happiness. Frankl suggests that humans can survive unimaginable suffering if they can locate meaning within it.

One line stayed with me long after finishing the book:

> “Those who have a 'why' to live, can bear with almost any 'how'.”
